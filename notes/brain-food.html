Brain Food

A selection of websites to stimulate your neurons.

  • Fucking Homepage
    Get new life pro tips, educational content, interesting websites, and more in the most vulgar way every day.
  • Khan Academy
    Over 3,000 videos covering everything from arithmetic to physics, finance, and history and hundreds of skills to practice.
  • Unplug the TV
    This website is meant to replace mind-numbing television. Instead of wasting your life watching TV, watch something mind-opening and educational.
  • Crash Course
    John Green covers world history and literature; Hank Green covers biology and ecology.
  • Minute Physics
    Simply put: cool physics and other sweet science.
  • SciShow
    Where the science goes.
  • TED
    Riveting talks by remarkable people, free to the world.
  • RSA Animate
    Animated talks on world-changing ideas.
  • No Excuse List
    The best place on the web to learn anything, for free. The list provides the best links to learn about academics, music, programming, languages and other useful resources.
  • How Stuff Works
    HowStuffWorks explains thousands of topics, from engines to lock-picking to ESP, with video and illustrations so you can learn how everything works.
  • BetterExplained
    Understand math, programming and other gnarly topics using intuition. Any concept can be made simple!
  • You Should Know
    A community to share obscure things that most people should already be aware of, but aren't.
  • Mental Floss
    Where knowledge junkies get their fix. Random, interesting, amazing facts - fun quizzes and trivia.
  • Letters of Note
    Correspondence deserving of a wider audience. Fascinating letters, postcards, telegrams, faxes, and memos.
  • MIT OpenCourseWare
    Web-based publication of virtually all MIT course content. OpenCourseWare is open and available to the world.
  • Colossal
    A blog that explores the intersection of art, design, and physical craft, specifically artwork that is tactile, physical and non-digital in nature.
  • Edge
    To arrive at the edge of the world's knowledge, seek out the most complex and sophisticated minds, put them in a room together, and have them ask each other the questions they are asking themselves.
